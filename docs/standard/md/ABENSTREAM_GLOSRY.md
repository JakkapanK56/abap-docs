Sequential succession of data records, the end of which cannot be predicted. Streams are divided into the [stream kinds](ABENSTREAM_KIND_GLOSRY.html)\\ [data stream](ABENDATA_STREAM_GLOSRY.html) and [filter stream](ABENFILTER_STREAM_GLOSRY.html). A stream is either a [reader stream](ABENREADER_STREAM_GLOSRY.html) or a [writer stream](ABENWRITER_STREAM_GLOSRY.html) and therefore always has exactly one [stream direction](ABENSTREAM_DIRECTION_GLOSRY.html). Furthermore, a distinction is made between the [binary stream](ABENBINARY_STREAM_GLOSRY.html) and [character stream](ABENCHARACTER_STREAM_GLOSRY.html). Multiple streams with matching properties can be combined into one stream. In ABAP, streams are implemented using [instances](ABENINSTANCE_GLOSRY.html) of special [classes](ABENCLASS_GLOSRY.html). The transfer of streams is called [streaming](ABENSTREAMING_GLOSRY.html).
