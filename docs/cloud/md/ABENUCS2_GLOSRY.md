Two byte [Unicode character representation](ABENUNICODE_CHAR_REPRESENT_GLOSRY.html). In this representation, all characters are represented by two bytes (16 bits). In UCS-2, 65536 different characters can be encoded, that correspond to the so called Basic Multilingual Plane (BMP) of the Unicode standard. In contrast to [UTF-16](ABENUTF16_GLOSRY.html), UCS-2 does not support characters from the [surrogate area](ABENSURROGATE_AREA_GLOSRY.html). The ABAP programming language supports UCS-2 in [Unicode systems](ABENUNICODE_SYSTEM_GLOSRY.html) and therefore no [surrogates](ABENSURROGATES_GLOSRY.html).
