This example demonstrates the different output behavior in groupings of internal tables.

In the [grouping](ABAPLOOP_AT_ITAB_GROUP_BY.html) of an internal table `itab`, the six ways of combining the [output behavior](ABAPLOOP_AT_ITAB_RESULT.html) of the `LOOP` statement with the [output behavior](ABAPLOOP_AT_ITAB_GROUP_BY_BINDING.html) of the [group loop](ABENGROUP_LOOP_GLOSRY.html) are demonstrated. The three [group loops](ABENGROUP_LOOP_GLOSRY.html) with [representative binding](ABAPLOOP_AT_ITAB_GROUP_BY_BINDING.html) and three group loops with [group key binding](ABAPLOOP_AT_ITAB_GROUP_BY_BINDING.html) are executed, each using the three ways of defining the [output behavior](ABAPLOOP_AT_ITAB_RESULT.html) of the `LOOP` statement.

In each group loop a [member loop](ABENMEMBER_LOOP_GLOSRY.html) is executed. Here, the group is specified after [`LOOP AT GROUP`](ABAPLOOP_AT_GROUP.html) that is produced by the output behavior. In the member loop, the members are placed in an internal table `members` using the [value operator](ABENVALUE_OPERATOR_GLOSRY.html) with the addition [`BASE`](ABENVALUE_CONSTRUCTOR_PARAMS_ITAB.html). This table is then output in the group loop. The result is the same in all cases.

In addition, the value of `sy-tabix` in the group loop is produced, which is different in the representative binding and in the group key binding. In the group key binding, the group key can also be addressed and produced, which is not possible in the representative binding.
